Movement

In TDIR, there are a few basics you'll need to know about to get started. We'll start with movement. Like many FPS games, you must move efficiantly and in a smooth way. TDIR also requires efficiant movement, or you're going to find yourself a smoking crater. In essence, most shooters are best controlled via a Mouse and Keyboard setup. Below is a link to setups that the Staff uses, feel free to try them or even mix and match them.


Staff's Configs

Essentially you'll want to remember that having Movement keys and Attacks in close positions, also things like Fly and Melee are going to be used frequently!


Another thing you'll need to make sure you do is rebind your Jump and Crouch keys! In TDIR they're no longer a part of JK's main key setup.



Flying

To fly, you'll need to bind a key to "Flight". Just like in previous DBZ Mods, you push the button to turn it on, and push it again to turn it off.



Charging Your Ki and Leveling up


Remember to bind a key to [Charge] and [Level Up]! When you run low on Ki, [Charge] will regenerate your ki at a faster rate than normal (Note: Ki only recharges while on the ground). When the Ki meter flashes yellow, you can push [Level Up] and your character will advance to the next level of power! If you hold down [Level Up] for a few seconds, then let go, you will power down. Power up again by tapping [Level Up]. Some characters (Like the Sayajins) will change their forms, while others will not! Be careful to see if your opponent is stronger than they look! (See PL Sense in Powers section)


Melee

In TDIR the Melee is performed by holding the [Melee] Key and Pushing one of the four directions (Forward, Back, Strafe Left, Strafe Right). Melee attacks can be modified via [Jump] or [Crouch], resulting in different speeds and strengths of attacks. Below is a link to the full list of attacks!

Ki Wave Attacks

Your character is provided 7 Basic forms of attacks. 6 Ki Wave attacks, and the Melee style which is described above. Each Ki Beam attack may be charged to 5 different power levels. Also, each beam may be controlled from your fixed position. Once the beam has fired, hold down the [Primary Fire] key and use your mouse to control the beam (Or look keys if you insist on using a keyboard). If you are locked onto someone, the beam will follow them. The below link will describe the Ki Wave Attacks.

Challanging opponents to one-on-one

Unlike other DBZ mods made for eye candy engines, TDiR has the most unique feature out of them all.   The challange button will allow you to fight a person one on one like a 3d fighter game.  You get a better view on your opponent and it's really fun.  What you do is lock on to the opponent you wish to challange and tap the challange button.  If you are challanged, press your challange button to accept and you will go into the challange mode.  You can take your opponent down any way you like, but be mindful that it'll get fast and furious.
